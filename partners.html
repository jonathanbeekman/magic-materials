The Challenge

Replacing plastic is harder than swapping a material.

Most companies assume a plastic replacement is a simple supplier swap. It isn't. PHA and PHB behave differently than conventional plastics — they require specialized formulation, different processing parameters, and manufacturing partners who understand how to work with them.

Building that expertise and those relationships from scratch takes years. Most companies don't have a materials science team. Most don't have existing relationships with PHA-capable manufacturers. And most can't afford to learn through failed production runs.

That's the gap we close.

What It Takes to Do This Right

Material science expertise. Understanding where PHA/PHB is and isn't appropriate — and how to formulate for specific performance requirements.

Formulation development. Custom blends designed for your product's flex, tensile, and processing requirements.

Manufacturing relationships. Partners who have run PHA/PHB through injection molding at commercial scale — not just in a lab.

Validation infrastructure. Third-party testing, certification pathways, and documentation that stands up to scrutiny.

The Material

Marine biodegradable and home compostable — genuinely.

Our products are made from Magic Materials PHA/PHB — a PHB-based biopolymer that biodegrades in marine water, home compost, and soil with no special facility required. This isn't industrial composting that most consumers can't access. It's real-world biodegradability.

Marine biodegradability is being validated in partnership with Washington State University under ASTM D6691 — the gold standard for ocean biodegradability testing. We don't make claims ahead of the science.
